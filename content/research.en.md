## Knowledge-Driven Intelligent Design Generation and Design Reasoning

This direction focuses on key scientific problems in the conceptual design of complex systems, including design intent modeling, knowledge representation, constraint reasoning, concept generation, and human-AI design support. The goal is to move design from experience-driven practice toward knowledge-driven and reasoning-supported processes, improving the explainability, generativity, and verifiability of conceptual design for complex systems.

### Design Intent Modeling and Knowledge Representation

This sub-direction studies how requirements, functions, behaviors, structures, and constraints are organized and represented in conceptual design of complex systems. It explores formal models of design intent and develops computable, inferable, and shareable frameworks for design knowledge representation.

### Constraint Reasoning and Concept Generation

This sub-direction investigates knowledge-driven reasoning and generative methods for conceptual design, with emphasis on constraint propagation, conflict resolution, candidate generation, and concept evolution. It supports the creation, comparison, and refinement of conceptual solutions for complex systems.

### Human-AI Design Support and Concept Evolution

This sub-direction studies the role of generative AI in supporting conceptual design. It focuses on collaboration between designers and intelligent systems, preservation of design intent, interactive reasoning, and concept evolution, with the aim of enabling high-quality conceptual design under human-AI collaboration.

## Multimodal Interaction and Supportive Design

This direction focuses on health promotion, capability development, disability support, and embodied interaction. It investigates multimodal sensing, interaction mechanisms, and supportive design approaches, with attention to how digital and physical interactive systems can better support human well-being, capability development, and engagement.

### Multimodal State Sensing and Recognition

This sub-direction investigates methods for sensing and recognizing human states through multimodal signals such as behavior, eye movements, facial expressions, voice, and physiological responses. It focuses on assessment and modeling of key states related to emotion, stress, workload, and capability performance in supportive contexts.

### Game-Based Interaction and Support Mechanism Design

This sub-direction studies the design of game-based tasks, feedback strategies, engagement rhythms, and motivational mechanisms in supportive contexts. It explores their application in emotional support, capability training, child development, and behavior facilitation.

### Supportive Embodied System Design

This sub-direction investigates the design of embodied interactive systems, physical devices, and related products for supportive contexts. It examines how interactive devices, assistive equipment, robots, exoskeletons, and other embodied systems can provide support through sensing, feedback, and physical interaction.
